25. Question : why is the design of taj mahal complicated?



The tomb is the main focus of the entire complex of the entire complex of Taj Mahal. Large, white marble structures stand on a square pedestal and arranged from a symmetrical building with iwan (a curved door) topped by a large and finial dome. Like the Mughal tomb, the basic elements of Persia. The basic structure is a large multi-chamber cube with chamfered corners forming an unequal eight-sided structure that is approximately 55 meters (180 feet) on each of the four long sides. Each side of the iwan is framed with a large pishtaq or a domed gate with two similarly shaped curved balconies stacked on both sides. This motif of stacked pishtaqs is replicated in a chamfered corner area, the design is completely symmetrical on all sides of the building. Four towers frame the tomb, one on every corner.
